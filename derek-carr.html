Derek Carr Education:

Carr began playing football on the school’s freshmen squad while attending the “Clements High School” in Sugar Land, Texas. He was a backup quarterback in his second season but requested to start the next year after another quarterback suffered a preseason injury.

He guided the Clements High School Rangers to a 13-0 record and an unbeaten season during his junior year. However, they were eliminated in the third round of the 2007 Class 5A Division 2 State Playoffs. During his junior year, he received scholarship offers from institutions.

Carr went to the “Bakersfield Christian High School” for his senior year. He guided the ‘Eagle’s’ squad to a 12-1 record, and they took home the Central Section Division V Championship of the California Interscholastic Federation. On October 29, 2008, “ESPN RISE” named him the “National Football Player of the Week,” and “The Bakersfield Californian” named him the “2008 All-Area Football Player of the Year.” After his senior year, Carr was chosen as the 2009 “National Player of the Year” by the “National Private Schools Athletic Association.”

Carr enrolled in the spring 2009 semester at “Fresno State University.” He sat out the 2010 campaign and joined the “Fresno State Squad” in the fall of 2011 as the starting quarterback. He played for his college team for three years. He won the title of “MWC Offensive Player of the Year” in 2012 and repeated as the honoree for the following two seasons, making him only the second player in league history to do so. He received the “2013 CFPA Elite QB Award” for his performance throughout his three seasons at the university. He was among the Mountain West Conference’s top performers in many statistical categories (MWC). He also took home the Sammy Baugh Trophy, given to the country’s best collegiate passer.

While there, he broke 21 “Mountain West Conference” records and 27 “Fresno State University” records. Carr’s outstanding academic performance earned him the title of “2013 National Football Foundation National Scholar-Athlete.” In the school’s history, he was just the third player to achieve this feat.

Derek Carr Career:

Carr was chosen by the “Oakland Raiders” as the 36th overall choice in the second round of the 2014 NFL Draft. He agreed to a $4.37 million, four-year deal in May 2014, including a $2.2 million signing bonus. He made NFL history by becoming the first rookie quarterback to start the Raiders team’s inaugural game. He was forced to exit the game in Week 4 of the season because of a high ankle sprain and an MCL sprain. On October 12, he broke the previous Raiders franchise mark for the most throwing touchdowns by a rookie in a single regular-season game with four.

Carr won his first NFL game in November 2014, as he helped the Raiders defeat the Kansas City Chiefs 24-20 and the San Francisco 49ers 24-13. He suffered a throwing hand injury at the start of the 2015 campaign, but he was able to play again by the second week and toss the game-winning touchdown against the “Baltimore Ravens.” He kept up his winning ways and, in January 2016, was selected for the “2016 Pro Bowl.”

Carr got his first “AFC Offensive Player of the Week” award in the eighth week of the 2016 campaign versus the “Tampa Bay Buccaneers.” He broke the record for most passing yards in a career (10,001) this year and guided the “Raiders” to their first playoff appearance since 2002.

He was selected for the “Pro-Bowl” for a second straight year on December 20, 2016. Due to an injury, he wasn’t able to compete in the Pro Bowl. Derek broke his fibula during a game on Christmas Eve, 2016, against the Indianapolis Colts,” and was unable to play for the “Raiders” for the remainder of the campaign. On the NFL Top Hundred Players of 2017, his other players placed him eleventh.

He agreed to a five-year, $125 million contract deal in June 2017 that included $70 million in total guarantees, $40 million in fully guaranteed payments, and a $12.5 million signing bonus. He rose to become the highest-paid player in the NFL, a title he held until Detroit Lions quarterback Matthew Stafford broke it in August 2017. The ‘Raiders’ 6-10 record in 2017 prevented them from making the playoffs. In January 2018, Carr was chosen for his third consecutive Pro Bowl.
